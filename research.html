  • Training for Manufactured Construction

    • Developed the course curriculum and the textbooks for four levels of the TRAMCON program, including Introductory, Basics, Advanced, and Supervisory. The program, which trains laborers in the manufactured housing industry and which is funded by a $9.9M grant from the U.S. Department of Labor, has been implemented in four Florida state colleges since 2016.

    Role: Graduate Assistant
    PIs: Kibert & Chini

    Publications:

  • Framework for Measuring Industrialized Construction Maturity

    • Researching the impact of emerging technologies on the future of industrialized construction, I conducted a series of projects, focusing on 1) identifying the state of emerging technology in the manufacturing industry which can be integrated in the industrialized construction process; 2) recognizing the barriers of utilizing these new technologies in the industrialized construction processes; 3) analyzing the performance of projects with high level of industrialization and modularization; and 4) developing a strategical framework for construction companies to use industrialized construction practices in the long term.

    Role: PhD researcher (dissertation)

  • Sustainability Evaluation of Infrastructure Projects

    • Researching the role of construction industry in achieving sustainable development goals, I developed a sustainability assessment framework for infrastructure projects. I used a questionnaire survey to ascertain economic, environmental, and social Key Performance Indicators identified from literature. I applied this framework in a case study for infrastructures in seaport and coastal areas.

    Role: MSc researcher (Thesis)

    Publications:
    • Razkenari, M. A., Nanehkaran, S. M., & Barati, K. (2016). Comprehensive Evaluation of Different Aspects of BIM Applications in Sustainable Design. J. Civ. Eng. Archit, 10, 1006-1014.
    • Sepasgozar, S. M., Razkenari, M. A., & Barati, K. (2015). The importance of new technology for delay mitigation in construction projects. American Journal of Civil Engineering and Architecture, 3(1), 15-20.
    • Razkenari, M. Mortaheb, M. M., (2015) Identify the Key Factors for Sustainability Assessment of Recreation and Passenger Ports. Proceedings of the 10th Project Management Conference. Tehran, Iran.

  • Key Elements of Net Zero Energy Buildings​

    • Analyzed data associated to Net Zero Energy (NZE) verified Buildings in the U.S. using R and Tableau software to evaluate and examine the key elements and features influencing the development of NZE buildings. ​
